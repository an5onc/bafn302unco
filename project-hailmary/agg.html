AGG Timeline

Report-style timeline showing how this holding fits into the broader five-year project story.

Jan 5, 2021

Project Buy Date

84.80 shares purchased at $117.92. AGG enters as the core U.S. investment-grade bond ETF — meant to be the "safe" anchor of the portfolio. 10-year Treasury yield: 0.93%.

Mar 16, 2022

Fed lift-off shatters bond math

First rate hike of a 425 bp cycle. Bond prices and yields move inversely — as yields ripped from 1.5% → 4.2%, AGG (≈6 yr duration) fell roughly 1×duration per 1% in yield = -13% on price alone.

2022 (full year)

Worst bond year in modern history

AGG -13% in 2022 — the worst calendar year for the U.S. Aggregate index since its 1976 inception. Stocks AND bonds fell together: the classic 60/40 portfolio's worst year since 1937.

Oct 23, 2023

10-year Treasury hits 5%

First time above 5% since 2007 — pushes AGG to its cycle low near $93. The pain is mostly price; coupon income (≈4-5%) keeps total return less negative than the price chart suggests.

Sept 18, 2024

Fed cuts 50 bp — AGG begins healing

First cut since 2020. Yields fall, bond prices rise; AGG slowly climbs back from the lows but never recovers cost basis within the project window.

Apr 27, 2026

Final tracking date — $99.44

AGG closes at $99.44, position worth $8,432.51 — the only holding still below its $10,000 cost (-15.67% total, -3.16% CAGR). The project's biggest lesson: "safe" is relative to the rate regime.

2022 correlation flip

The 60/40 broke

For decades, bonds zigged when stocks zagged. In 2022, both fell together — destroying the diversification math the textbook 60/40 portfolio is built on. AGG's drawdown taught the lesson live.

Mar 10, 2023

SVB collapse, AGG rallies

Silicon Valley Bank fails on duration mismatch — owning long bonds bought at 0% rates. Flight-to-safety bid pushes AGG +3.5% in a week. Bonds resume their "haven" role briefly.

2024–2026

Coupon does the heavy lifting

With AGG yielding 4-5%, the position earned meaningful interest income — but our "ending value" only reflects price. The real total return (price + reinvested coupons) is closer to -6% than -16%.

Reflection Draft

AGG may have had the weakest return, but it is still one of the most valuable positions for analysis. It teaches that diversification does not guarantee gains in every sleeve at the same time, especially when rates rise.
