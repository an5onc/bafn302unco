Tesla (TSLA) Timeline

Key dated events and price context showing how Tesla traded through the five-year holding period.

Jan 5, 2021

Project Buy Date — $245.04

The Tesla position begins with 40.81 shares at $245.04. Tesla had already split 5-for-1 in August 2020, and the stock entered 2021 with strong momentum coming off a massive 2020 rally.

Nov 4, 2021

All-Time High — ~$414

TSLA reached its split-adjusted all-time high of approximately $414 in early November 2021. Investor enthusiasm for EVs, Tesla's inclusion in the S&P 500, and strong delivery growth all contributed. At this peak, the $10,000 position would have been worth roughly $16,900.

Nov 6–8, 2021

Musk Twitter Poll Triggers 25% Drop

Elon Musk ran a Twitter poll asking followers whether he should sell 10% of his Tesla holdings. When the "Yes" vote won, Musk sold approximately $15 billion in Tesla stock over the next several weeks. TSLA fell more than 25% from its peak by mid-December 2021.

Aug 2022

3-for-1 Stock Split + Full-Year Collapse

Tesla executed a 3-for-1 stock split in August 2022. Despite the split, the stock had fallen more than 65% from its January 2022 price by year-end — from roughly $380 to under $125. The Fed raised rates by 425 basis points in 2022, compressing high-growth valuations, while Musk's $44 billion acquisition of Twitter in October raised additional concerns about his focus and finances.

Jan 2, 2025

First Annual Delivery Decline in Company History

Tesla reported Q4 2024 results, bringing full-year 2024 deliveries to approximately 1.79 million vehicles — the first year-over-year annual delivery decline in Tesla's history. Combined with Musk's visible role heading the Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE) under President Trump, the stock fell from near $480 at the start of 2025 to below $250 by late February 2025.

Apr 27, 2026

Progress Report Checkpoint — $378.56

Tesla closed at $378.56, bringing the position to $15,449.03. The stock recovered from its early-2025 lows, supported by renewed investor interest in Tesla's autonomous driving and robotaxi strategy.

Event 1 · Nov 6, 2021

Musk Twitter Share Poll

Elon Musk tweeted a poll asking if he should sell 10% of his Tesla stake to pay taxes. The poll received over 3.5 million votes and Musk honored the result, selling approximately $15 billion in shares over the following weeks. TSLA fell from its all-time high of ~$414 by more than 25% in just six weeks — a direct lesson in how a single executive decision can quickly reshape a stock's trajectory.

Event 2 · 2022

Fed Rate Hikes Crush TSLA by 65%

The Federal Reserve raised the federal funds rate from near zero to 4.25–4.50% over the course of 2022 — the sharpest rate-hike cycle in decades. Growth stocks with high valuations were hit hardest, and Tesla was no exception. TSLA fell from approximately $380 in January 2022 to below $125 by December 2022 — a 67% decline in a single calendar year. Musk's $44 billion Twitter acquisition in October 2022 added further concern about his financial priorities.

Event 3 · Jan 2, 2025

First Annual Delivery Decline

For the first time in Tesla's history, full-year vehicle deliveries declined year-over-year in 2024. The miss sparked a sharp sell-off, with TSLA dropping from roughly $480 to below $250 by late February 2025. Musk's high-profile role in the Trump administration's DOGE initiative raised questions about how much time he was dedicating to Tesla. The stock gradually recovered through 2025, closing at $378.56 at the April 2026 project checkpoint.

Reflection Draft

Tesla was useful in the project because it showed how a single stock can behave very differently from a diversified fund. It still delivered a gain, but it required much more confidence and patience than the ETFs.
