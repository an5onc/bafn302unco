Paige's Reflection

What I personally took from running this portfolio for 5.25 years.

I picked BRK.B mostly because Buffett is the name everyone in finance class hears about, but I didn't really expect it to compete with QQQ. BRK.B more than doubled to +107.85%, beating SPY and VUG and crushing my teammates' Tesla and EA picks — the strongest individual stock in the entire project. The only thing that beat it was QQQ at +113.02%, and even that was close. It made me realize Berkshire isn't really a single stock — it's basically its own diversified portfolio of insurance, railroads, energy, and equities all packaged into one ticker.

What stood out about 2022 was how differently BRK.B behaved compared to QQQ. Growth stocks got crushed by Fed hikes, but BRK.B's insurance float (which earns more when rates are higher) and energy holdings actually benefited from the same environment. Two equities, same year, opposite outcomes — that taught me more about asset-class behavior than any chapter in the textbook.

The Buffett succession to Greg Abel didn't even move the stock much in 2025, which honestly impressed me. The institution is bigger than any one person now. If I built a real portfolio someday, BRK.B is the only individual stock I'd seriously consider holding — everything else, I'd probably let an ETF (especially QQQ) do the work.
