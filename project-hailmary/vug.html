VUG Timeline

Report-style timeline showing how this holding fits into the broader five-year project story.

Jan 5, 2021

Project Buy Date

238.80 shares purchased at $41.88. VUG enters as Vanguard's broad U.S. growth ETF — ~190 holdings vs. QQQ's 100, with lower expense ratio (0.04% vs 0.20%).

Nov 8, 2021

VUG all-time high near $329

Peak of the growth trade alongside QQQ. Same growth-style risk factors, but slightly more diversified across mid-cap growth names.

2022

Growth-style drawdown of -33%

Same DCF math hit as QQQ — long-duration cash flows discounted harder as the 10-year Treasury yield ran from 1.5% to 4.2%. VUG bottoms in October 2022.

Q1 2024

Growth-style leadership returns

AI capex narrative and the start of disinflation push large-cap growth back to leadership. VUG benefits from heavy weight in Microsoft, Apple, Nvidia, and Alphabet (combined ~30% of the fund).

2025

Tracks QQQ closely but trails slightly

VUG's broader 190-holding basket dilutes the AI super-cycle exposure vs. QQQ's pure Nasdaq-100 concentration. Final spread: ~14 percentage points behind QQQ.

Apr 27, 2026

6-for-1 stock split announced

Vanguard executes a 6-for-1 split on VUG (alongside VGT 8-for-1 and MGK 5-for-1). Pre-split price ≈ $500 → post-split ≈ $83.49. Total position value is unchanged: 238.80 new shares × $83.49 = $19,937.41 — same as 39.80 old shares × $500.94. The split simply makes the per-share price more accessible to new investors.

Apr 27, 2026

Final tracking date — $83.49

VUG closes at $83.49 (post-split), position worth $19,937.41 — +99.37% total return, 13.89% CAGR. Beat SPY by ~7 percentage points, trailed QQQ by ~14 — third-best performer in the project.

Sept 13, 2022

August CPI shocks growth stocks

Hotter-than-expected 8.3% inflation print sends VUG -5.4% in a single session — its worst day of the cycle. Confirms long-duration growth's rate-sensitivity.

Dec 13, 2023

Fed pivot — VUG ignites

Powell's "dot plot" signals 75 bp of 2024 cuts. VUG rallies +6.5% over the next two weeks; growth-style outperformance resumes for all of 2024.

2025

VUG vs. QQQ gap widens

QQQ's tighter 100-stock concentration in AI mega-caps outperforms VUG's broader 190-stock basket — a textbook lesson on the trade-off between concentration and diversification within a style.

Reflection Draft

VUG might be the easiest growth fund to defend in the project because it delivered a strong return while still being more diversified than Tesla. It supports the case for growth without full single-stock risk.
