Mikey's Reflection

What I personally took from running this portfolio for 5.25 years.

I picked EA because I actually play their games — Madden, FC, Apex — and I wanted to "invest in something I understand," like the Peter Lynch advice. EA delivered a solid +43.25% total return — but it actually trailed Tesla (+54.49%) by about 11 points, and the bigger lesson was that EA lost to QQQ by about 70 percentage points. Knowing the product isn't the same as having an investing edge.

What surprised me was 2022. EA and AGG both fell at the same time, for completely different reasons — EA on the FIFA-license breakup uncertainty and a tough console-cycle year, AGG on the Fed's 425 bp of hikes wrecking long-duration bonds. Two unrelated risks, but they hit my portfolio together. That's what "correlation going to one in a crisis" actually looks like on a real account.

If I did this again I'd still own EA, but I'd be honest with myself: it's the entertainment-budget portion of the portfolio, not the core. The serious money should sit in QQQ, SPY, and VUG, where the long-run math actually works in my favor.
