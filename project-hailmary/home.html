Group Portfolio Comparison

Each member started with $50,000 on January 5, 2021. This chart shows where each portfolio stood at the April 27, 2026 checkpoint.

About This Project

  • Start date: January 5, 2021 (first trading Tuesday of 2021).
  • Amount per position: $10,000 — five positions per student, $50,000 total each.
  • Shared holdings: SPY, QQQ, VUG, and AGG appear in every portfolio at the same buy price and share count.
  • Individual stocks: Anson tracks Tesla (TSLA), Mikey tracks Electronic Arts (EA), and Paige tracks Berkshire Hathaway (BRK.B).
  • End date: April 27, 2026 — the project's final tracking date (presentation day).

What the Numbers Show

  • All three portfolios grew substantially from the $50,000 starting point, driven by the strong performance of QQQ, VUG, and SPY.
  • Paige's portfolio was the strongest — BRK.B more than doubled, adding significant value on top of the shared ETF gains.
  • Anson's and Mikey's portfolios finished about $1,100 apart. Tesla (+54.49%) outperformed EA (+43.25%) over the full period, putting Anson narrowly ahead.
  • QQQ took the top spot in every portfolio at $21,301.86 (+113.02%) — even beating Paige's BRK.B (+107.85%), the strongest individual stock pick. The "boring" growth ETF won.
  • AGG was the only holding below its starting value in all three portfolios, illustrating how rising rates affected bonds differently than stocks.
