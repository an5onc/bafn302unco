Anson's Reflection

What I personally took from running this portfolio for 5.25 years.

What surprised me most was that SPY beat my Tesla pick by nearly $3,800 on the same $10,000 starting capital — and SPY did it with no single-company headline risk. I picked Tesla because I wanted the "big swing" position, and the price action did deliver excitement: a 65% drop in 2022, the Twitter/X distraction in late 2022, then a partial recovery on the robotaxi narrative. But the boring index fund quietly compounded past it. And QQQ beat SPY too — Tesla finished 4th out of 5.

The other thing I didn't expect: AGG losing money for the entire 5+ year hold. Going in, I assumed bonds were the "safe" leg. 2022 broke that assumption — when the Fed hiked 425 bp in 18 months, both my stocks AND my bonds fell together. The 60/40 portfolio I'd read about in textbooks had its worst year since 1937, in real time, on my own statement.

If I ran this again, I'd still keep Tesla as the speculative sleeve — but I'd size it smaller and let the index funds (SPY/QQQ/VUG) carry more of the portfolio. The single-stock excitement isn't worth giving up the steady compounding that diversification delivered.
