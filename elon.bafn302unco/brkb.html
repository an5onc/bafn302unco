Berkshire Hathaway (BRK.B) Timeline

Key events affecting Berkshire's business, stock, and the five-year tracking period.

Jan 5, 2021

Project Buy Date

The BRK.B position begins at $227.47 per share (NYSE closing price) with 43.96 shares, representing a $10,000 simulated investment. Berkshire enters the tracking period with a massive cash position and a diversified portfolio of businesses and equities.

2021–2023

Capital Allocation and Buybacks

Berkshire deployed capital steadily through share buybacks and select acquisitions during this period. The company's insurance operations (GEICO, Berkshire Hathaway Reinsurance) and operating businesses — including BNSF Railroad and Berkshire Hathaway Energy — contributed stable earnings.

2023–2024

Record Cash Position and Large Equity Holdings

Berkshire's cash reserves grew to record levels as Buffett reduced certain equity holdings. Annual reports continued emphasizing patience, intrinsic value, and long-term capital allocation — consistent with Berkshire's decades-long philosophy.

2025

Warren Buffett Steps Down as CEO

Warren Buffett announced he would step down as CEO, with Greg Abel — the Vice Chairman of Non-Insurance Operations — succeeding him. The announcement was widely anticipated and reflected years of succession planning. Abel has been deeply embedded in Berkshire's operations and is expected to continue Buffett's value-oriented approach.

Jan 2026

OxyChem Acquisition Completed

Berkshire completed its acquisition of OxyChem (Occidental Chemical Corporation) in January 2026, deepening its relationship with Occidental Petroleum and expanding its presence in chemicals and energy infrastructure. This followed Berkshire's significant Occidental equity stake built up since 2022.

Event 1

Warren Buffett Succession (2025)

Buffett announced he would step down as CEO with Greg Abel taking over. This represented the end of a legendary era in corporate history. Markets responded with measured confidence, as Abel had been Berkshire's operational leader for years and the company's culture and investment approach are deeply institutionalized.

Event 2

OxyChem Acquisition (Jan 2026)

Berkshire completed the OxyChem acquisition in January 2026, expanding its energy and chemicals footprint. Berkshire had been building its Occidental Petroleum stake since 2022 and this acquisition of the chemicals subsidiary reflected its continued conviction in the energy sector and preference for tangible, capital-intensive businesses.

Event 3

Capital Allocation Strategy

Berkshire's recent annual reports have consistently emphasized holding a large cash position, executing selective buybacks when shares trade below intrinsic value, and deploying capital into "elephant-sized" acquisitions when opportunities arise. This disciplined approach is a core reason BRK.B has consistently outperformed over long time horizons.

Company Overview

Industry: Diversified Financial / Holding Company

Sector: Financials

Dividend: No — Berkshire prefers reinvesting earnings over paying dividends. Shareholders received only one cash dividend long ago; the company has not paid one since.

Berkshire Hathaway is not a traditional single-industry stock. It operates as a massive conglomerate with fully owned subsidiaries in insurance (GEICO, General Re), railroads (BNSF), energy (Berkshire Hathaway Energy), manufacturing, retail, and services, alongside a large portfolio of publicly traded equity investments. This internal diversification means BRK.B behaves somewhat like a fund — but one managed by one of the most disciplined capital allocators in history.
