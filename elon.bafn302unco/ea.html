Electronic Arts (EA) Timeline

Key events affecting EA's business, stock, and the five-year tracking period.

Jan 5, 2021

Project Buy Date

The EA position begins at $141.32 per share (NASDAQ closing price) with 70.76 shares, representing a $10,000 simulated investment.

2021–2022

Gaming Boom and Normalization

Post-pandemic gaming engagement remained elevated in 2021, but as life normalized in 2022, the industry faced a reset. EA navigated this with its live-service titles and recurring sports franchises.

2022

FIFA Partnership Ends — EA SPORTS FC Launches

EA announced the end of its 30-year FIFA licensing partnership and rebranded its flagship soccer franchise as EA SPORTS FC. The move was seen as a long-term positive for brand control and margin, though it created short-term uncertainty.

Jan 2025

Bookings Forecast Cut

EA cut its full-year bookings guidance in January 2025, citing disappointing performance in certain titles. The stock declined sharply on the news, highlighting execution risk for a company heavily dependent on game launches and live-service engagement.

Feb 2026

Q3 FY26 Results and Dividend Declaration

EA released its Q3 FY26 results in February 2026 and declared a quarterly cash dividend of $0.19 per share, payable March 18, 2026. This reinforced EA's commitment to returning capital to shareholders.

Event 1

End of FIFA Partnership (2022)

After 30 years, EA and FIFA split over licensing costs and brand control. EA rebranded its soccer franchise as EA SPORTS FC, retaining the game engine, player licenses, and live-service infrastructure. Long-term, this gave EA full control over its most popular franchise and removed a costly royalty structure.

Event 2

Bookings Forecast Cut (Jan 2025)

EA lowered its full-year bookings forecast in January 2025, surprising investors and causing a notable stock decline. The revision reflected weaker-than-expected engagement with several key titles and highlighted how closely EA's valuation is tied to game release timing and live-service performance.

Event 3

Q3 FY26 Results & Dividend (Feb 2026)

EA reported Q3 FY26 results in February 2026, maintaining its quarterly dividend of $0.19 per share. The results showcased ongoing live-service revenue resilience and confirmed EA's position as a stable, dividend-paying entertainment company — an uncommon trait in the gaming sector.

Company Overview

Industry: Interactive Entertainment / Video Games

Sector: Communication Services

Dividend: Yes — $0.19/share quarterly as of February 2026

EA is one of the largest video game publishers in the world, with a portfolio spanning sports (EA SPORTS FC, Madden NFL, NHL), shooters (Battlefield, Apex Legends), and lifestyle titles (The Sims). Revenue is increasingly driven by live-service digital spending, microtransactions, and subscription services like EA Play. The company's model is more recurring and subscription-like than a pure one-time game publisher, which provides some revenue stability even in softer launch years.
