Why This Matters

Compound interest is the foundation of wealth building. When you invest money, not only do you earn interest on your principal, but you also earn interest on that interest. This compounding effect accelerates growth dramatically over time, especially with frequent compounding periods and longer time horizons.

What each input means:
  • Principal (P) — The initial amount of money you invest or borrow
  • Interest Rate (r) — The annual interest rate expressed as a percentage
  • Time (t) — The number of years the money is invested or borrowed
  • Compounding Frequency (n) — How often interest is calculated and added (Annual=1, Semi=2, Quarterly=4, Monthly=12, Daily=365)
