Gordon Growth Model (Dividend Discount)

💡
Why This Matters

The Gordon Growth Model (Constant Growth DDM) values a stock as the present value of all future dividends growing at a constant rate. It's the foundation of intrinsic value analysis for dividend-paying firms.

Formulas:
  • P₀ = D₁ / (r − g) — intrinsic stock price
  • D₁ = D₀ × (1 + g) — next dividend
  • r = D₁/P₀ + g — required return
  • g = r − D₁/P₀ — implied growth rate
  • r must be > g, and both must be positive
