WACC — Weighted Average Cost of Capital

💡
Why This Matters

WACC is the hurdle rate a firm must beat with any investment. It blends the cost of debt and equity, weighted by their proportions in the capital structure, after accounting for the tax deductibility of interest. A project with NPV > 0 at WACC creates shareholder value; NPV < 0 destroys it.

What each input means:
  • Market Value of Equity (E) — Total equity market cap (shares × price)
  • Market Value of Debt (D) — Total market value of all interest-bearing debt
  • Cost of Equity (rₑ) — Required return for equity investors (use CAPM: rf + β·MRP)
  • Pre-Tax Cost of Debt (rᵈ) — YTM on existing debt (not coupon rate)
  • Tax Rate (T) — Marginal corporate tax rate (interest expense is tax-deductible)

Formula: WACC = (E/V)·rₑ + (D/V)·rᵈ·(1 − T), where V = E + D

HP 10bII+ — No Direct WACC Key

WACC is a manual formula. Use your calculator for the component rates (CAPM for rₑ, YTM for rᵈ), then punch in:

1. Compute weights:  w_E = E ÷ (E + D)    w_D = D ÷ (E + D)
2. After-tax rᵈ:    rd_at = rᵈ × (1 − T)
3. WACC:            WACC = w_E × rₑ + w_D × rd_at

All rates should be in decimal form when calculating manually (e.g. 12.5% → 0.125).
